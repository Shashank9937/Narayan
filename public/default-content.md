## FOUNDATION PRINCIPLES

### The Only Metrics That Matter
- **Month 0-3:** Customer conversations per week (target: 25+)
- **Month 4-6:** Weekly Active Users (WAU) or Revenue
- **Month 7-9:** Month-over-Month Growth Rate (target: 15%+)
- **Month 10-12:** ARR or GMV run-rate (target: $2M-4M for strong Series A)

### Core Truths
1. **Ideas are worthless, execution is everything** - You'll pivot 2-3 times
2. **Speed compounds** - 80% done today > 100% done next month
3. **Traction forgives all sins** - Growth solves fundraising, hiring, everything
4. **Talk to users obsessively** - 5 convos/day minimum during validation
5. **Burn = runway = time = only resource** - Optimize for time, not money saved

## IDEA GENERATION ENGINE

### Method 1: Personal Expertise + Market Gaps
**Process:**
1. List 10 industries you know deeply
2. For each: What's broken? What wastes time? What's manual?
3. Cross-reference with: AI trends, regulatory changes, new behaviors

**Best For:** B2B SaaS, vertical software, professional tools

---

### Method 2: Trend Intersection
**Formula:** [Emerging Tech] x [Proven Business Model] x [Growing Market]

**Examples:**
- AI x LinkedIn x Recruiting = AI recruiting platform
- Blockchain x Uber x Logistics = Decentralized delivery
- Computer Vision x Shopify x Manufacturing = Visual quality control

**Process:**
1. List 5 emerging technologies
2. List 10 proven business models
3. List 10 growing markets
4. Create 50 combinations
5. Evaluate each

---

### Method 3: Services-to-Software
**Process:**
1. Find fragmented service industries (legal, accounting, recruiting, etc.)
2. Identify manual, repetitive tasks
3. Build software to automate or scale

**Best For:** B2B, high-margin SaaS, clear ROI

---

### Method 4: Copycat + Localize
**Process:**
1. Find successful models in other markets
2. Adapt to: Different geography, industry, or customer segment
3. Execute faster and better

**Examples:**
- Uber for X -> Every vertical
- Stripe for Y -> Every geography or use case

---

### Method 5: Pain Mining
**Process:**
1. Join 10 subreddits, Slack groups, forums in target industries
2. Search for: "I hate", "frustrated", "wish there was"
3. Document 50 painful problems
4. Rank by frequency and intensity

**Best For:** Consumer apps, PLG products

---

### Method 6: Follow the Money
**Process:**
1. Find industries with high spend on X
2. Build: Better/cheaper/faster X
3. Capture: Even 1% of existing spend = huge market

**Examples:**
- Enterprise spend $200B on salespeople -> Sales automation tools
- Companies spend $50B on customer support -> Support AI

## IDEA EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

### SCORE EACH IDEA (1-10 scale):

**1. Market Size (10 = $10B+, 1 = <$100M)**
- TAM: Total addressable market
- SAM: Serviceable addressable market
- SOM: Serviceable obtainable market (3-year target)
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 7+

**2. Personal Advantage (10 = unfair advantage, 1 = none)**
- Domain expertise (worked in industry)
- Technical skills (can build it yourself)
- Distribution (existing audience or network)
- Insight (non-obvious truth you know)
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 6+

**3. Timing (10 = perfect timing, 1 = too early/late)**
- Why now? What changed recently?
- Regulatory, tech, or behavior shift
- Competition: Too early (0 competitors) or too late (100+)?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 7+

**4. Problem Intensity (10 = hair on fire, 1 = nice to have)**
- How painful is current solution?
- Is this a vitamin or painkiller?
- Do customers actively search for solutions?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 8+

**5. Monetization Clarity (10 = obvious, 1 = unclear)**
- Clear willingness to pay?
- Existing budget line item?
- ROI or value easily quantifiable?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 7+

**6. Time to Revenue (10 = <3 months, 1 = >18 months)**
- Can you pre-sell before building?
- Self-serve or high-touch sales?
- Long sales cycles kill momentum
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 7+

**7. Competitive Moat Potential (10 = strong moat, 1 = commodity)**
- Network effects, data, brand, or switching costs?
- Defensibility in year 3?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 6+

**8. Customer Acquisition (10 = easy/cheap, 1 = hard/expensive)**
- Defined customer segment?
- Obvious channels to reach them?
- Organic/viral potential?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 7+

**9. Founder-Market Fit (10 = perfect fit, 1 = no fit)**
- Would you use this product?
- Do you know 50+ potential customers?
- Are you obsessed with this problem?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 8+

**10. Venture Scale Potential (10 = unicorn possible, 1 = lifestyle biz)**
- Path to $100M+ ARR?
- Can this be a category leader?
- VC-backable business model?
- **PASS THRESHOLD:** 7+

### DECISION RULE:
- **Total Score 75+** -> Strongly consider
- **Total Score 65-74** -> Possibly, but risky
- **Total Score <65** -> Pass, keep searching

### MUST-PASS TESTS:
Even with high score, KILL if:
- X Market size <$1B TAM
- X Problem intensity <8 (people won't pay)
- X Founder-market fit <7 (you'll quit)
- X No personal advantage (you'll get outcompeted)

## VALIDATION MACHINE

### PHASE 1: PROBLEM VALIDATION (Week 1-2, Target: 25 interviews)

**Interview Script Template:**

**Introduction (2 min)**
- "I'm researching [problem space]. You're not a customer, just helping me learn."
- "This will take 15 minutes."
- "Can I record this?"

**Current State (8 min)**
- "Walk me through how you currently [do X]"
- "What's frustrating about that?"
- "What have you tried to solve this?"
- "Why didn't that work?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"

**Ideal Future (3 min)**
- "If you had a magic wand, what would the perfect solution look like?"
- "What features are must-have vs nice-to-have?"

**Buying Intent (2 min)**
- "If I built this, would you be interested?"
- "Would you pay for this?"
- "How much?" (let them answer first)
- "Can I follow up when we have something?"

**VALIDATION CRITERIA:**
- âœ“ 80%+ confirm this is a real, painful problem
- âœ“ 50%+ express strong willingness to pay
- âœ“ Consistent patterns in what they want
- âœ“ You can clearly articulate: Who, What pain, Why now

---

### PHASE 2: SOLUTION VALIDATION (Week 3-4, Target: 20 interviews)

**Artifacts Needed:**
1. Mockups (Figma, PowerPoint, or paper)
2. Product demo script
3. Pricing options
4. Simple pitch deck

**Demo Script:**

**Setup (2 min)**
- "Based on our last conversation, we're building [solution]"
- "I want to show you what we're thinking and get feedback"

**Walkthrough (10 min)**
- Show mockups/prototype
- Explain key workflows
- Point out: "This solves [pain point we discussed]"
- Watch their face (excitement or confusion?)

**Feedback (5 min)**
- "What do you like? What's missing?"
- "Would this actually solve your problem?"
- "What would make this a must-have for you?"

**Close (3 min)**
- "We're launching in 4 weeks to first 10 customers"
- "Would you be one of them?"
- "Here's the pricing: [options]"
- "Can I put you down for [package]?"

**VALIDATION CRITERIA:**
- âœ“ 50%+ show genuine excitement
- âœ“ 25%+ commit to buy (verbal or written)
- âœ“ 5+ actually pay deposit or full price
- âœ“ Feedback is specific and actionable

**IF FAIL:**
- <25% excitement -> Wrong solution or wrong customers
- <5 paying -> Problem not painful enough or price too high
- Vague feedback -> You're talking to wrong people
- **ACTION:** Pivot solution OR pivot customer segment OR kill idea

### PHASE 3: DEMAND VALIDATION (Week 4, Budget: $500)

**Landing Page Test:**

**Page Must Include:**
1. Clear headline (value prop in 10 words)
2. 3 key benefits (outcome-focused)
3. Social proof (even if "joining 500+ on waitlist")
4. Email capture form
5. Clear CTA

**Ad Test Formula:**
- Audience: Hyper-specific (not "small business owners" -> "dentists in California with 2+ locations")
- Budget: $500 split across 5 ad variations
- Duration: 5-7 days
- Goal: Email signups

**SUCCESS METRICS:**
- Landing page conversion: 5%+ -> Good fit
- Cost per lead: <$5 -> Cheap acquisition
- Email quality: 50%+ open rate on follow-up -> Right audience

**IF FAIL:**
- <2% conversion -> Value prop unclear or wrong audience
- >$20 cost per lead -> Acquisition will be too expensive
- <20% email engagement -> Wrong people signing up
- **ACTION:** Rewrite value prop OR change targeting OR kill idea

## MVP EXECUTION ROADMAP

### PHILOSOPHY: MVP = Minimum VIABLE Product
- Viable = Customers get value and pay
- Not minimum features
- Not pretty
- Not scalable
- JUST: Does it solve the core problem?

---

### MVP SCOPING FRAMEWORK

**Step 1: Define the Core Job**
- What is the ONE thing this product must do?
- Example: Uber = "Get car to pick me up"
- Example: Stripe = "Accept payment on website"
- **Your core job: ___________**

**Step 2: User Story Mapping**
List steps user takes to complete core job:
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. ...

**For each step, ask:**
- Can this be manual? (You doing it behind scenes?)
- Can this be automated later?
- Is this core or nice-to-have?

**Step 3: Ruthless Feature Cut**
Start with: Every feature idea
Cut: Everything that's not essential to core job
Keep cutting: Until you can build it in 4 weeks

**The Test:**
- If you remove this feature, does the product still solve the problem?
- If yes -> CUT IT
- If no -> Keep it

**Step 4: Tech Stack Decision**

**Decision Tree:**
- Can you pre-sell and deliver manually? -> Do that (Week 1-2)
- Can you use no-code (Webflow, Airtable, Zapier)? -> Do that (Week 2-3)
- Must you code? -> Use fastest framework you know

**Best Stacks for Speed:**
- Full-stack: Next.js + Supabase + Vercel
- Mobile: React Native + Firebase
- No-code: Webflow + Airtable + Zapier/Make
- AI app: OpenAI API + Python + Streamlit

**DON'T:**
- Learn new framework for MVP
- Optimize for scale (you'll pivot anyway)
- Overthink architecture

---

### 4-WEEK MVP BUILD SPRINT

**Week 1: Foundation + Manual MVP**
- Day 1-2: Set up dev environment, basic app structure
- Day 3-4: Build simplest possible user auth
- Day 5: Create basic UI (copy existing designs, use Tailwind/shadcn)
- Day 6-7: Manually deliver service to 2 customers (proves value without full product)

**Week 2: Core Workflow**
- Day 8-10: Build main user flow (happy path only)
- Day 11-12: Add most critical feature (#1 from user feedback)
- Day 13-14: Internal testing, fix broken stuff

**Week 3: Make It Usable**
- Day 15-16: Improve onboarding (biggest ROI)
- Day 17-18: Add payments (Stripe, LemonSqueezy)
- Day 19-20: Basic error handling, loading states
- Day 21: Polish most important screens

**Week 4: Launch**
- Day 22-23: Final testing with real users
- Day 24-25: Deploy, monitor, fix critical bugs
- Day 26-28: Onboard first 5 paying customers
- **Launch Day: Get 5 customers using it**

### PLAYBOOK 1: COLD EMAIL (B2B)

**Week 1: Setup**
- Buy email tool: Apollo, Instantly, Smartlead ($50-100/mo)
- Set up: Custom domain, warm up email (3-5 days)
- Build list: 500 ICPs (ideal customer profiles)

**Week 2: Test**
- Write: 5 different cold email templates
- Test: 100 emails each (500 total)
- Track: Open rate, reply rate, meeting rate

**Week 3: Optimize**
- Kill: Templates with <5% reply rate
- Scale: Best template to 1000 emails/week
- Target: 2-5% reply rate, 25%+ meeting conversion

**Week 4: Systematize**
- Document: Best templates, follow-up sequences
- Hire: VA to build lists, manage responses ($500/mo)
- Scale: 2000+ emails/week

**Good Email Template:**
```
Subject: [First Name], quick question about [pain point]

Hi [First Name],

Noticed [Company] is [growing/hiring/raising/specific observation].

Are you still [doing manual process] for [specific task]?

We help [similar companies] [specific outcome] in [timeframe].

Worth 15 min?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Metrics to Hit:**
- Open rate: 40%+
- Reply rate: 3-7%
- Meeting rate: 20-30% of replies
- Close rate: 15-25% of meetings

### PLAYBOOK 2: CONTENT MARKETING (B2B/B2C)

### PLAYBOOK 4: PRODUCT-LED GROWTH (PLG)

**Requirements:**
- Self-serve product
- Low/no-touch sales
- Viral potential

**Implementation:**

**Step 1: Optimize Onboarding**
- Time to "aha moment" < 5 minutes
- Show value before asking for anything
- Interactive tutorial, not video
- Track: % of signups who complete core action

**Step 2: Build Virality**
- Referral program: Give $X, get $Y
- Social sharing: "I just [outcome] with [product]"

- Multiplayer features: Collaboration requires invites

**Step 3: Conversion Optimization**
- Freemium: Free tier -> Paid upsell
- Free trial: 14 days -> Convert before ending

- Target: 2-5% free-to-paid conversion

**Step 4: Retention Loops**
- Email: Re-engagement campaigns
- In-app: Notifications for unused features
- Content: Weekly tips, best practices
- Community: User forums, shared wins

**Key Metrics:**
- Viral coefficient: >1.0 (each user brings 1+ more)
- Activation rate: >40%
- Free-to-paid: >3%
- Net revenue retention: >100%

**Week 3-4: Term Sheets and Negotiation**

**Term Sheet Key Terms:**

**Economics:**
- **Valuation:** Pre-money or post-money?
- **Investment amount:** How much cash?
- **Pro-rata rights:** Can they invest in next round?

**Control:**
- **Board composition:** Founders, investors, independent?
- **Protective provisions:** What requires investor approval?
- **Voting rights:** How are decisions made?

**Liquidation:**
- **Liquidation preference:** 1x non-participating (standard) vs participating (bad for you)
- **Anti-dilution:** Weighted average (fair) vs full ratchet (bad for you)

**Other:**
- **Option pool:** % reserved for employees (comes from founder shares)
- **Drag-along:** Can investors force sale?
- **No-shop:** How long are you locked in?

**Negotiation Strategy:**
1. **Get multiple term sheets** (FOMO = leverage)
2. **Don't optimize for valuation** -> Optimize for: partner quality, terms, speed
3. **Ask for references** -> Talk to other founders they've invested in
4. **Push back on bad terms** -> Participating preferred, >1x liquidation preference, full ratchet
5. **Get legal counsel** -> Hire experienced startup lawyer ($500-1000/hr but worth it)

**Decision Framework:**
- **Tier 1 investor + fair terms + strong support** -> Take it
- **Tier 2 investor + great terms + okay support** -> Maybe
- **Tier 3 investor + bad terms + poor support** -> Pass (even if you're desperate)

## DECISION FRAMEWORKS

### FRAMEWORK 1: THE PIVOT DECISION

**When to Consider Pivoting:**
- No traction after 3 months of execution
- Can't get to $10K MRR in 12 months
- Customer feedback consistently negative
- You've lost conviction

**The Pivot Matrix:**

| | Same Customer | Different Customer |
|---|---|---|
| **Same Problem** | Tweak solution, pricing, positioning | Probably wrong problem |
| **Different Problem** | Customer pivot (easier) | Full pivot (hardest) |

**Decision Process:**
1. **Data check:** What do metrics say?
2. **Customer check:** What do 10 customers say?
3. **Gut check:** Are you still excited?
4. **Market check:** Is there a there there?

**If 3/4 are negative -> Pivot**
**If 2/4 are negative -> Give it 2 more weeks, then decide**

---

### FRAMEWORK 2: HIRING DECISIONS

**Should You Hire for This Role?**

**The ROI Test:**
- What revenue/value will this person generate?
- Is it 5x their cost?
- If not: Don't hire, outsource instead

**Hire When:**
- You've done the job yourself and understand it
- You have repeatable processes to hand off
- Revenue or funding supports it
- It's a high-leverage role

**Don't Hire When:**
- You're just overwhelmed (hire help, not employees)
- Role is unclear
- Can't afford 18 months of salary
- You haven't proven product-market fit

**First 5 Hires (in order):**
1. **Engineer** (if you're non-technical founder)
2. **Sales/BizDev** (to free you up)
3. **Customer Success** (to retain customers)
4. **Second Engineer** (to ship faster)
5. **Marketing/Growth** (to scale acquisition)

---

### FRAMEWORK 3: FEATURE PRIORITIZATION

**The RICE Score:**
- **Reach:** How many users will this impact? (1-1000+)
- **Impact:** How much will it improve their experience? (1-3)
- **Confidence:** How sure are you? (0.5-1.0)
- **Effort:** How many person-weeks? (0.5-10+)

**Score = (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort**

**Example:**
- Feature A: (1000 x 3 x 0.8) / 2 = 1200
- Feature B: (100 x 3 x 1.0) / 0.5 = 600
- Build Feature A first

---

### FRAMEWORK 4: PRICING DECISIONS

**Value-Based Pricing:**
1. What's the economic value to customer?
2. Charge: 10-20% of that value
3. Or: 3x your cost to deliver

**Price Testing:**
- Test: Increase price by 20% for new customers
- Measure: Conversion rate change
- If conversion drops <10% -> Keep new price
- Repeat until conversion drops >20%

**Pricing Models:**
- **Usage-based:** Pay for what you use (AWS, Twilio)
- **Tiered:** Good/Better/Best (Slack, HubSpot)
- **Flat rate:** One price, unlimited use
- **Per seat:** Per user pricing (Zoom, Figma)

**Best for Early Stage:**
- Start simple: Flat rate or basic tiers
- Test aggressively: Change prices monthly
- Optimize: For revenue, not conversion rate
